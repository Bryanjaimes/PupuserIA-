## 1. ü™ô BITCOIN RESERVE & POLICY

### Bitcoin as Legal Tender (2021‚Äì2025)
- **[CONFIRMED]** El Salvador adopted Bitcoin as legal tender on **September 7, 2021** via the **Bitcoin Law** ‚Äî the first country in the world to do so.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Government purchased first **200 BTC** on September 6, 2021.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Between September 2021 and January 2022, the government spent approximately **$85.5 million** on Bitcoin.
- **[CONFIRMED]** On **January 29, 2025**, the Bitcoin Law was **amended** ‚Äî Bitcoin was **rescinded as legal tender** but still allowed as a means of payment.

### Current Bitcoin Holdings
- **[CONFIRMED]** As of **January 19, 2025**, El Salvador held **6,043.18 BTC** worth approximately **$611,219,629** (announced by Bukele via social media).
- **[CONFIRMED]** On December 19, 2024, the government purchased **11 BTC** worth over $1 million ‚Äî one day after the IMF deal.

### National Bitcoin Office
- **[CONFIRMED]** The **National Bitcoin Office (ONBTC)** exists and operates in El Salvador.
- **[CONFIRMED]** **Max Keiser** serves as personal advisor to Bukele on cryptocurrency matters.
- **[CONFIRMED]** **Stacy Herbert** (Keiser's wife) is employed by the government of El Salvador and has claimed the role of director.
- **[CONFIRMED]** On December 19, 2024, Stacy Herbert stated El Salvador would continue buying Bitcoin at an **"accelerated rate"** ("ritmo acelerado").

### IMF Deal (December 2024)
- **[CONFIRMED]** On **December 18, 2024**, the IMF agreed to a **$1.4 billion loan** to El Salvador.
- **[CONFIRMED]** El Salvador agreed to concessions:
  - No mandatory business acceptance of Bitcoin
  - No Bitcoin for tax payments
  - Reduced government Bitcoin purchases
- **[CONFIRMED]** The day after the deal, the government purchased 11 BTC anyway.

### Chivo Wallet
- **[CONFIRMED]** The government launched the **Chivo Wallet** app for Bitcoin transactions.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Usage declined sharply: **25.7% (2021) ‚Üí 21% (2022) ‚Üí 12% (2023) ‚Üí 8.1% (2024)**.

### Volcano Bonds
- **[CONFIRMED]** "Volcano Bonds" ‚Äî $1 billion Bitcoin-backed bonds ‚Äî were announced in November 2021 to fund Bitcoin City.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Issuance was **postponed in March 2022**, citing global conditions and the Ukraine war.
- **[UNCONFIRMED]** Current status of Volcano Bonds as of early 2026 unclear from available sources.

### Citizenship-for-Bitcoin
- **[CONFIRMED]** In **December 2023**, a law was passed allowing foreigners to purchase Salvadoran citizenship via Bitcoin donation.

- **[CONFIRMED]** State of Exception declared **March 27, 2022** in response to 87 murders over 3 days.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Extended **47 times** as of **January 29, 2026** ‚Äî still ongoing.
- **[CONFIRMED]** As of January 29, 2026: **94,844+ people arrested**; 8,000+ released.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Suspended constitutional rights: freedom of association, right to counsel; detention without charge extended from 3 to 15 days.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Prison sentences increased: gang leaders 40‚Äì45 years (from 6‚Äì9); members 20‚Äì30 years (from 3‚Äì5).
- **[CONFIRMED]** Age of criminal responsibility reduced from 16 to **12 years old**.
- **[CONFIRMED]** 91% domestic approval rating (April 2022 Gallup poll).
- **[CONFIRMED]** Homicide rate dropped from 103 per 100,000 (2015) to record lows by 2024.
- **[CONFIRMED]** 496 homicides registered in 2022, a **56.8% decrease** from 1,147 in 2021.
- **[CONFIRMED]** El Salvador has the **highest incarceration rate in the world** as of 2023.
